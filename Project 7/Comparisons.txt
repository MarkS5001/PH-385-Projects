I investigated different cubic grids for the Ising model

I used the following sizes (run time, beta value, critical temperature):
-10 (12s, 0.43, 2.25)
-20 (140s, 0.24, 2.25)
-30 (382s, 0.21, 2.25)
-40 (1645s, 0.19, 2.25)
-50 (1732s, 0.19, 2.25)

When grid size in increases the drop to zero magnetization 
gets more pronounced. A grid size of 30 seems to be where 
magnetization settles the most after the critical temperature.
The beta value also hovers around 0.2, with the critical
temperature remaining the same for all iterations.
